Privacy & Trust Issues

How AI affects your personal information

AI systems need lots of personal data to work well, which brings up privacy concerns. Our research shows many AI systems collect too much data without telling users clearly.

Jeremy Peckham's research suggests that AI could cause serious problems if we trust these systems too much. There's worry that AI might hurt privacy, freedom, and even relationships as we rely more on these technologies.

The trust issue goes beyond just privacy - as AI makes more important decisions, we need to ask who's responsible when something goes wrong.

"Humans are special because we're made by God, which makes us different from machines, so AI shouldn't control us, but the other way around."

â€” Jeremy Peckham, as cited in Perspectives on Science and Christian Faith

Jobs & Money

How AI is changing work

Our research shows that AI could affect over 300 million jobs worldwide, according to Goldman Sachs data from 2023. In Canada, some jobs will change more than others.

While some old jobs are going away, AI is also creating new types of jobs. However, the change is causing problems for workers trying to adapt and for keeping the economy stable.

The Canadian government is working on this problem through its AI Strategy, which includes training programs and support as industries change with more AI use.

"Over 300 million jobs around the world could be affected by AI."

â€” Goldman Sachs, 2023
